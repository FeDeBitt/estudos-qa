# Software Quality Characteristics (ISO/IEC 25010)

**Context:** This project was developed as a practical exercise to deeply understand the fundamental Software Quality Characteristics. To demonstrate these concepts in a tangible way, I analyzed an everyday object (a pencil) through the lens of software quality standards.

## Analyzed Item: Pencil
A pencil is a portable item used for writing, drawing, or sketching. It looks like a straight and thin stick, filled with graphite or colored clay, typically surrounded by a wooden case. Upon applying pressure against a paper sheet, the core starts wearing down, and the writing or drawing comes to life.

---

### 1. Functionality
* **Suitability:** The pencil must be able to perform its main task, which is graphite marking on flexible and solid surfaces.
* **Accuracy:** The line must be precise and consistent with the applied pressure, allowing thin writing and shading as well.
* **Safety:** The pencil must adhere to safety protocols, making sure that the materials (graphite, wood, rubber) are not toxic.
* **Additional functionality:** By adding a rubber on its end, it is possible to erase markings.

### 2. Reliability
* **Maturity:** The composition of the graphite and wood must assure it will withstand pressure applied during use and the sharpening process without premature flaws.
* **Fault Tolerance:** The wooden case must be able to absorb impact. In case of accidental drops, the structure must protect the inner core.
* **Recoverability:** In case of breaking the pencil's tip, it must be able to restore its functional state with sharpening.

### 3. Usability
* **Operability:** The pencil's shape must have a comfortable grip and avoid rolling on flat surfaces. The weight must be equally distributed so as not to fatigue the user's hand.
* **Learnability:** The design must be intuitive, allowing every user to identify immediately how to operate the item without further instructions.
* **User Interface Aesthetics:** The external finish must be smooth and pleasant to the touch, ensuring that the visual and tactile aspects contribute to a positive experience.
* **Accessibility:** The pencil's diameter and length must be standardized to adjust to both adults' and children's hands, as well as being compatible with standard sharpeners on the market.

### 4. Efficiency
* **Time behaviour:** The graphite must allow smooth and continuous writing, without flaws in pigment that might oblige the user to remake the same movement.
* **Resource utilization:** The pencil must maximize durability, ensuring a high word count per centimeter of graphite used. Wood waste on sharpening must be the minimum possible to prolong the item's lifespan.
* **Capacity:** The graphite must make legible traces even with minimum pressure, allowing the user to save material when making sketches or quick notes.

### 5. Maintainability
* **Analyzability:** The user must easily identify when the pencil needs sharpening.
* **Modifiability:** The pencil must be able to be sharpened numerous times without losing resistance. If there's an eraser, it must be functional until the end of the pencil's lifespan.
* **Testability:** It must be easy to verify if the pencil is ready for use after sharpening.

### 6. Portability
* **Adaptability:** The pencil must be able to function in different environments without deforming the wood or degrading the graphite.
* **Installability:** The pencil must have a standard shape and size, allowing its storage in pencil cases and pockets.
* **Replaceability:** The pencil must be easily replaced by another with the same specifications without compromising the user's technique.
